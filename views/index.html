Scenes from this week

Today, you are an astronaut. You are floating in inner space 100 miles above the surface of Earth. You peer through your telescope and this is what you see. You are people watching. These are fleeting moments.

These videos come from YouTube. They were uploaded some time in the past week and have titles like DSC 1234 and IMG 4321. Most have fewer than five (5) views. They are unnamed, unedited, and unseen by anyone but you.

The controls are simple. If you like a video and wish it not to switch, you can press the hold button—. If a video switches before you’ve a chance to press hold, you can press the back button—— which will return to and hold the previous video. You can return to the live stream by pressing the live button—. You will also notice a share button——located to the right of the video controls. This allows you to share the presently playing video.

As a convenience, use the following keyboard shortcuts: The down arrow maps to the hold button; the left arrow maps to the back button; and the right arrow maps to the live stream button. Pressing m will mute the video.

For those of you with friends in faraway places, you can take advantage of Astronaut’s Global Synchronization Capability. Have your friend open Astronaut on their own computer. You’ll be pleased to find that their live stream is synchronized with yours. Enjoy a shared moment.
